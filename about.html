My Story

I am currently completing my senior year at Georgia State University, pursuing a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science. My interest in programming comes from wanting to build things that actually work in real situations, not just for assignments. I actively look for opportunities to apply what I learn through personal projects and hands-on practice.

I value clean structure and good habits when it comes to coding. One lesson I’ve learned over time is that it’s better to build something simple, test it, and improve it step by step rather than waiting for everything to be perfect from the start. This approach has helped me stay consistent and make steady progress.

Outside of academics, I enjoy fitness and maintaining a structured routine. Staying consistent in my daily habits has made a big difference in how I manage coursework, projects, and long-term goals. I’ve found that discipline outside of coding translates directly into better focus and productivity when working on technical problems.

Right now, my main focus is strengthening my fundamentals, including HTML, CSS, programming structure, and writing readable, maintainable code. I’m especially interested in developing skills that scale well over time and prepare me for real-world software development environments.

Skills & Interests

Below are some of the technical areas I have experience with and actively continue to develop through coursework and personal projects:

  • HTML: Writing clean, semantic markup with accessibility and structure in mind
